Ideal Job: Senior Software Engineer

With software engineer ranked 11th in the 2018 Burning Glass data, having 539 job listings my selection for this as my ideal job remains strongly in place. Some of my existing skills are very highly ranked in this data set which justifies that I am on the right trajectory for my career path. With further knowledge of programming that I will gain from my work experience and this university degree I believe that I will be in a place to fill a position such as this one within a few years time. I am looking forward to more experience with group work and collaboration which has also been identified as top skills that are sought after by companies hiring for positions such as this one.
I expect job numbers for this role to grow exponentially over the coming years as the world moves towards a more technologically advanced future. There will soon be job titles we could never have imagined in this field just as there are jobs now that 20 years ago would have seemed impossible. I am looking forward to advancements in AI paving the way for these sorts of roles. My hope is that I will be able to find a position on the forefront of technology advancement upon completion of this degree.
To see creativity and problem solving being ranked so highly for these positions is extremely exciting as I believe creative problem solving is what makes software development so satisfying. Coming up with new ways to solve problems and having the freedom to use creative methods in the vast world of code is the main reason I enjoy what I do.

Ideal Job: Junior Software Developer

While the role of Junior Developer doesn't rank near the top in terms of demand determined by the number of job ads it is still in enough demand with 66 job ads placing it at 134 in the list over 200. The demand for the other more experienced roles may not something that can be applied for immediately after completing a degree in IT. Without the necessary experience it will be very difficult to position yourself and any of your potentially limited skill sets to an employer. I had chosen the junior Developer role as a natural stepping stone on the beginning of my journey through the IT industry. The general skills required such as communication skills, teamwork, problem solving and creativity and their rankings are inline with objectives and learning outcomes of this very unit.
The IT skill sets required for a junior role, let alone a senior role, rank well in terms of demand from potential employers. The highly sort after IT-specific skills that are on the top of the list yet but may not be sought after by employers of a junior role are SQL, Microsoft Windows and project management. While I currently have no experience with SQL I do have broad experience with the other two and would be confident expressing and demonstrating that to any potential employer. With the general skills required the top of the list is organisational skills, writing and troubleshooting. Again, with my years employed as both a project manager and operations manager am I confident I would be able to adequately demonstrate these skills to any potential employer.
Do I have higher aspirations? Absolutely. Currently my major interests lie in UNIX based operating systems and free, open source software projects. But due to previously being employed in management roles I am also aware that i need to work my way up to my goals through experience and I am more than prepared to do so.

When broken down, my required skill set encourages me even more to explore this career path. It is in high demand, the specialised and general skills are too, I intend to pursue this further.

Ideal Job: Maritime Warfare, Submariner Commander

The demand for submarine commanders directly limited by the amount of submarines Australia owns and operates. The 2016 Defence Whitepaper sets out the long-term plan for the Australian Defence Force, in the paper the government has committed to doubling the number of submarines it will have, from 6 Collins-class submarines to 12-Attack-class submarines. The new submarines are expected to start construction in 2022 and start entering service from 2032. This increase in submarines will require an increase the required people to man and command them and works perfectly with my plan to start getting experience in the job. By the end of the 2030’s I should have enough experience and qualifications to become a commander as the new submarines are still entering service.
Since promotion in the Navy is competitive between candidates, my experience with diesel systems as a heavy-vehicle mechanic should give me an edge as the new submarines are diesel-electric powered and also my time in the army has developed my ability to think as a combatant and can directly be applied to using combat systems. Teamwork and leadership however are not my greatest skills and I will have to work on developing these professional tools.
Some of the specialized skill sets required for my ideal job is very niche and can only be gained through experience as a junior officer in the Navy, however I already have some of the generic skills and experience that can transfer over to some of the specialized skills. I am enthusiastic about transitioning into this career once I have completed my studies.
